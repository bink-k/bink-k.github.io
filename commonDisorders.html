Common Disorders + Treatments

The Most Common Mental Disorders Teens Have

Note there is a large range of mental disorders amongst teens, these are just few.

Generalized Anxiety Disorder

Excessive, ongoing anxiety about several different areas which becomes hard to control.

Symptoms : Restlessness, easily fatigued, muscle tension, struggling with sleep, decline in grades

Social Anxiety

Everyday interactions lead to significant anxiety due ot the fear of being judged by others.

Symptoms : Feeling anxious around others, avoiding places where people will be, struggling to keep friendships

Depression

Persistent feelings of sadness, loneliness, anxiety interfering with daily activities

Symptoms: Feeling persistently sad, feeling worthless, losing interest in hobbies, a decline in grades, unexplained changes in appetite or weight

**Please don’t try to diagnose a teen as a parent or a teacher. If a teen in your life appears to have a mental illness please make an appointment with their primary care doctor first as they have the teen's medical history to ensure a correct diagnosis.**

Common Treatments for these Disorders

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy

Most commonly used to treat anxiety and depression (although it could be used for other disorders), this type of therapy manages one’s problems by changing how one thinks/behaves.

Medication

Most commonly selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors are usually used to treat anxiety and depression.

Support Groups

When battling a mental illness, seeking people that are facing the same struggles prevents people from feeling alone.

Self-Care

Although self-care alone can’t cure a mental disorder, caring for one’s mental health can help. This involves exercising daily, eating healthy, listening to music, or using coloring books.
